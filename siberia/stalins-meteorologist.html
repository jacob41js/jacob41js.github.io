Stalin’s Meteorologist

Author: Olivier Rolin

Date Read: May 2025

Pages: 178

★★★☆☆
3/5

Reading Status: Complete

Siberia Gulag Soviet History Biography

Personal Reflections

Unfortunately, I cannot say this book had much to do with Siberia. The story was about a meteorologist working on weather modeling that was to contribute to the great communist undertaking of revamping the USSR’s agricultural system to make it more productive and able to feed a massive empire, who was ultimately unjustly arrested, probably as a result of a jealous colleague looking to make his way to the upper echelons of the agency during Stalin’s purges. Alexey Feodosievich Wangeheim was imprisoned in a gulag on Archangelsk and lived and toiled in a workcamp for several years. Over the course of his imprisonment he wrote letters to his daughter documenting the things he saw through sketches and descriptions, still finding beautiful and interesting things in the bleakest conditions. Ultimately, though, despite having his wife attempt to appeal to Stalin himself, he was murdered along with 100 of his fellow prisoners one night after sailing to the opposite shore of the White Sea near Kem.

Although a beautiful piece of writing, even translated from French, I can’t say I took much from this book. Most of it I read while on vacation, onboard the Disney Magic or an airplane traveling to and from Orlando, and my most vivid memory will probably be the half hour of solitude I got while on the ship where I curled up in a porthole window couch and cracked open this book. The scene was quite romantic.

I will also likely remember this book as an errant recommendation from AI when I set out to create a syllabus for myself on Siberia. This perhaps only glanced on Siberia in that in the mind of nearly everybody that territory is synonymous with the gulag system, and this book drove home the terror, cruelty, remoteness, and isolation of those camps. Would not recommend, but certainly will be putting it on my shelf in the Siberia section because it does have a nice cover design.
